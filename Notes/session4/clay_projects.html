Projects

The following section contains short introductions to the projects for this course. All projects investigate adsorption onto a clay mineral for a variety of applications. Clays constitute a significant part of soils and sediments, and due to their high specific surface area and charge, they play an important role in the retention and migration of environmental pollutants.

All projects in this course are dedicated to examining the adsorption onto clay minerals. These minerals are a significant constituent of soils and sediments. Their high specific surface area and cation exchange capacity enables them to engage effectively with a variety of molecules and ions. Such capabilities are crucial in the context of environmental pollution control, where clay minerals play a vital role in the adsorption and immobilisation of contaminants, thereby influencing their migration and bioavailability in soils and aquatic systems.

Furthermore, the inherent adsorptive properties of clay minerals extend their applicability beyond environmental remediation. In pharmaceutical formulations, their ability to adsorb therapeutic agents and release them in a controlled manner can be used to control drug release kinetics. Another application of clay minerals is in the field of hazardous waste management, particularly concerning nuclear waste. They are employed to prevent the dispersal of radioactive materials into the environment and act as a natural barrier to contain and isolate contaminants.

pH-dependent adsorption of apigenin onto montmorillonite

Introduction

This project investigates the pH-dependent adsorption of apigenin (APG) onto montmorillonite (MMT) clays. APG is a naturally occurring flavonoid of bright yellow colour has been used in natural dyes. It is only poorly degraded in soil and plays an important role in microbe-plant communication. Depending on the pH of the environment, it can be present in four different protonation states (Figure 1), which can affect its adsorption behaviour. The project aims to investigate the adsorption of APG onto MMT clays at different pH values and to determine the effect of pH on the adsorption capacity and mechanisms.

Suggested reading

  • https://doi.org/10.1021/acs.jpcc.3c00174

Tasks

All required files for this project are located inside the practicals/projects/project_A folder.

The following list provides a general description for the tasks of this project. You can find the details on the different project options in the Options section below.

  1. Copy the project_A folder to your home directory.
  2. Use the MMT unit cells to assemble a clay sheet.
    All clay unit cells can be found inside the CLAYS folder.
    The dimensions should be around 3.5 × 5.0  nm.
  3. Extend the simulation box to 100 Å in the z-direction.

  4. Add the number of APG molecules to the simulation box that corresponds to a concentration of 0.1 mol L-1.
    Use the prevalent protonation state at the pH-value of your option.

    Table 1: Protonation states of APG (see figure 1) and corresponding structure files.
    protonation state structure file
    Api0 api2
    Api- api0
    Api2- api3
    Api3- api4
  5. Solvate the box with SPC water.
  6. Add 0.1 mol L-1 of the ion combination specified in your option and neutralise any excess charge. If your cation has a charge different from +1, you need to add the -pq flag to specify the cation charge.
  7. Perform an energy minimisation with a force tolerance of 500 kJ mol-1 nm-1.
  8. Equilibrate the system for 100 ps in the NVT ensemble at 300 K.
  9. Equilibrate the system for 100 ps in the NPT ensemble at 300 K and 1 bar.
  10. Run a 20 ns production simulation in the NPT ensemble at 300 K and 1 bar.
  11. Determine the point where the system reached equilibrium.
  12. Calculate the linear density of the APG molecules.
  13. Calculate the linear density of the clay sheet.
  14. Calculate the linear density of the added cation species.
  15. Calculate the linear density of the Cl- ions.
  16. Open all linear density results in xmgrace and save the plot in .jpeg format.
  17. Inspect the final coordinates and trajectory in VMD and take a snapshot that illustrates interactions between APG and the clay sheet.

Useful information

Calculating the number of organic molecules to reach a certain concentration

To calculate the volume of the simulation box that is occupied by the solvent. The height of the solvent layer can be obtained by subtracting the height of the clay unit cell from the total height of the expanded simulation box.
Remember to keep the units of the volume and the concentration to be consistent in your calculations.

Calculating the linear density of organic molecules

Use gmx density to calculate the linear densities of molecules or ions along the z-axis.
Usage:

gmx density ‐f <trajectory> ‐s <tpr file> ‐n <index file> ‐o <output file> ‐d z ‐sl <number of slices> ‐ng 2 ‐dens number ‐b <beginning time> ‐e <end time>>

‐f specifies the input trajectory file (.xtc or .trr)

‐s specifies the input .tpr file

‐n specifies the input index file (.ndx)

‐o specifies the output file (.xvg)

‐d specifies the direction of the density calculation

‐sl specifies the number of slices along the density profile. Setting it to 1‐2 times the box height in Å usually gives a good resolution.

‐ng specifies the number of groups to calculate the density for

‐dens specifies the type of density to calculate (number, mass, charge, etc.)

‐b specifies the beginning time of the trajectory to consider

‐e specifies the end time of the trajectory to consider

Before running gmx density you will need to use gmx make_ndx to create an index file with the atoms of interest.

Input files

Organic molecule structures and molecule definitions are located inside the STRUCTURES folders of each project. Force fields are inside the FF and clay unit cells inside the CLAYS folders. Run parameter .mdp files are inside MDP. Use em.mdp for energy minimisation, nvt.mdp for equilibration at constant temperature and volume, npt.mdp for equilibration at constant tempoerature and pressure and prod.mdp for the production run. Make sure to fill in any missing values for the keywords of the .mdp files that are marked with COMPLETE HERE.

You will find template scripts for submitting your simulation on eddie inside the project folder.
Remember to run all of your simulations on eddie!
