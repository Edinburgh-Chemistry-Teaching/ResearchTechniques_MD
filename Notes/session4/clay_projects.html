Projects

The following section contains short introductions to the projects for this course. All projects investigate adsorption onto a clay mineral for a variety of applications. Each project consists of multiple options, with each option requiring you to perform and compare two simulations. Rank your preference for the projects using this form, and we will assign you to a project option on a first come, first served basis. We will email you your assigned project during the session.

Clay minerals are a significant constituent of soils and sediments. Their high specific surface area and cation exchange capacity enables them to engage effectively with a variety of molecules and ions. Such capabilities are crucial in the context of environmental pollution control, where clay minerals play a vital role in the adsorption and immobilisation of contaminants, thereby influencing their migration and bioavailability in soils and aquatic systems. In pharmaceutical formulations, their ability to adsorb therapeutic agents and release them in a controlled manner can be used to control drug release kinetics. Another application of clay minerals is in the field of hazardous waste management, particularly concerning nuclear waste. They are employed to prevent the dispersal of radioactive materials into the environment and act as a natural barrier to contain and isolate contaminants.

pH-dependent adsorption of apigenin onto montmorillonite

Introduction

This project investigates the pH-dependent adsorption of apigenin (APG) onto montmorillonite (MMT) clays. APG is a naturally occurring flavonoid of bright yellow colour has been used in natural dyes. It is only poorly degraded in soil and plays an important role in microbe-plant communication. Depending on the pH of the environment, it can be present in four different protonation states (Figure 1), which can affect its adsorption behaviour. The project aims to investigate the adsorption of APG onto MMT clays at different pH values and to determine the effect of pH on the adsorption capacity and mechanisms.

Tasks

All required files for this project are located inside the practicals/projects/layered_material/project_A folder. The following list provides a general description for the tasks of this project, which you should carry out for each of your systems.

  1. Copy the project_A folder to your Desktop.
  2. Use the MMT unit cells to assemble a clay sheet.
    All clay unit cells can be found inside the CLAYS folder.
    The dimensions should be around 3.5 × 5.0 nm in x and y.
  3. Extend the simulation box to 50 Å in the z-direction.

  4. Add the number of APG molecules to the simulation box that corresponds to a concentration of 0.1 mol L-1.
    Use the prevalent protonation state at the pH-value of your option.

    Table 1: Protonation states of APG (see figure 1) and the corresponding structure files.
    pH Protonation state Structure file
    5 Api0 api1
    7 Api- api2
    8 Api2- api3
    10 Api3- api4
  5. Solvate the box with SPC water.
  6. Add 0.1 mol L-1 of the ion combination specified in your option and neutralise any excess charge. If your cation has a charge different from +1, you need to add the -pq flag to specify the cation charge.
  7. Perform an energy minimisation with a force tolerance of 500 kJ mol-1 nm-1.
  8. Equilibrate the system for 100 ps in the NPT ensemble at 300 K and 1 bar.
  9. Run a 50 ns production simulation in the NPT ensemble at 300 K and 1 bar.
  10. Determine the point where the system reaches equilibrium. Use only the equilibrated portion for subsequent analysis.
  11. Calculate the linear density of all components.
  12. Determine the hydration shell of the ions.
  13. Calculate the RDF of the interactions between the ions and the charged sites on the organics.
  14. Use xmgrace to plot your results and save them in .jpeg format.
  15. Inspect the final coordinates and trajectory in VMD and take snapshot(s) that illustrate the important interactions within the system.
  16. Compare and discuss your simulations, providing illustrations from your analysis and VMD as needed.

Adsorption of radioactive ions onto montmorillonite and kaolinite

Introduction

Radioactive ions are of interest due to their potential release from nuclear waste repositories and their potential migration into the environment. The project aims to investigate the adsorption of radioactive Cs+, Rb+, Sr2+, and Ba2+ and Ra2+ ions onto montmorillonite (MMT) and kaolinite (KAO) clays.
KAO is a neutral non-swelling clay which is distinct from other clays due to its structure. It has one tetrahedral siloxane surface that is hydrophobic, and another octahedral aluminium hydroxide surface that is hydrophilic.

Suggested reading

  • Molecular simulation on Cs, Rb retention in Na/K-montmorillonite interlayer coupling clay swelling/collapse
  • Adsorption of barium and radium on montmorillonite: A comparative experimental and modelling study
  • Adsorption and Desorption Behaviors of Sr on Montmorillonite: A Triple Sr Isotope Perspective

Useful information

Input files

Organic molecule structures and molecule definitions are located inside the STRUCTURES folders of each project. Force fields are inside the FF and clay unit cells inside the CLAYS folders. Run parameter .mdp files are inside MDP. Use em.mdp for energy minimisation, nvt.mdp for equilibration at constant temperature and volume, npt.mdp for equilibration at constant temperature and pressure and prod.mdp for the production run. Make sure to fill in any missing values for the keywords of the .mdp files that are marked with COMPLETE HERE.

You will find template scripts for submitting your simulation on eddie inside the project folder.
Remember to run all of your simulations on eddie!
